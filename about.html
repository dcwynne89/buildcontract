About BuildContract

I built BuildContract after watching too many contractor-client relationships go sideways over scope. The work was fine. The communication was fine. But nobody wrote down what was actually agreed to — so when the project shifted, both sides had different memories of what was included.

A verbal agreement is just a misunderstanding that hasn't happened yet. A written contract doesn't mean you don't trust someone — it means you both value clarity enough to spend 3 minutes documenting it.

What makes a good contract? Four things: a clear scope of work, defined payment terms, a timeline, and two signatures. Miss any one of those and you've got a proposal, not a contract. BuildContract structures all four by default.

The scope section uses numbered clauses instead of free-text paragraphs. That's deliberate — when a dispute comes up, you can point to "Clause 3" instead of arguing about what a paragraph meant. Structured scope is enforceable scope.

BuildContract is part of a small ecosystem of free tools for independent work: BuildQuotes for estimates, BuildInvoice for billing, BuildChangeOrder for scope changes, BuildPDF for file conversion, and BuildCertificates for certificates. No account needed for any of them.
